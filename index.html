Linear Regression

Linear regression is one of the most widely used and foundational tools in statistics and machine learning. It serves as a gateway to understanding more complex modeling techniques and provides essential insights into the nature of relationships between variables. This chapter begins with a simple intuitive visualization of data, and step by step, builds the mathematical formulation of the linear regression model. We focus on the probabilistic interpretation of the model, the derivation of the maximum likelihood estimators, and the assumptions that underlie the method.


The goal is not just to present formulas but to offer a deeper understanding of why linear regression works the way it does, what its limitations are, and how it connects with broader concepts in data analysis and statistical inference. Whether the reader is a student encountering linear regression for the first time, or a practitioner looking to revisit the fundamentals, this chapter aims to offer a balanced blend of theory, intuition, and practical considerations.
